# Kamereo Interview — Answers

---

## Q1: Who are Kamereo's target customers?

Kamereo is a B2B food supplier for F&B businesses, so its customers are domestic F&B companies. The original segment was HORECA — restaurants, hotels, and cafés such as Pizza 4P's and El Gaucho. From there it expanded into modern retail (supermarkets, convenience stores), enterprise/institutional clients (Starbucks, factories, schools, hospitals), and the long-tail SME segment — small restaurants.

---

## Q2: What specific needs or challenges does Kamereo address for our customers?

Kamereo solves the pain points a restaurant faces every day: having to deal with many small, informal suppliers, unstable pricing, a lack of valid VAT invoices, no traceability of origin, and unreliable delivery. Kamereo consolidates all of this into a single chain: from logistics (on-time delivery) to complete invoicing to farm-to-table traceability (an on-time delivery system running from Đà Lạt to HCMC). The crucial point is that Kamereo controls every one of these pain points, so it creates value that isn't dependent on the ups and downs of the market.

---

## Q3: What are Kamereo's primary product categories?

Kamereo is a one-stop platform. The main groups are: fresh produce (washed and pre-cut cores); protein (fresh meat and seafood); dry goods, pantry, and beverages (spices, sauces, milk, drinks); and non-food items (toilet paper, napkins, cleaning supplies, cling film). The strategy running through all of this is one-stop: bundling everything a restaurant or household needs to order daily into a single supply chain, with traceable origins and food safety enabled by owning its own farm and delivering same-day.

---

## Q4: What key performance metrics does Kamereo focus on to drive growth?

On the customer-service/operational side: on-time delivery rate, error-free products, and order-fill (fulfillment) rate.
On the B2B growth side: sales is the headline KPI, which can be broken into four parts — new sales and net revenue retention cover account breadth, while order count and average order value cover account depth.

---

## Q5: What factors contributed to Kamereo's successful capital raise, particularly during the challenging funding environment?

The key point here is why Kamereo could raise funds when other companies couldn't. It's because Taku Tanaka had built relationships with investors well before the challenging funding environment arrived. By meeting them face-to-face and understanding each other on a personal level (personal relationships) rather than cold pitching (Zoom calls), he reassured investors that backing Kamereo was a risk-off, long-term, profitable investment. Another factor here is operational discipline — focusing on what can be controlled rather than chasing the macroeconomy — which made the company more resilient during the challenging funding period and COVID.

---

## Q6: What are Kamereo's new product categories, and what are the reasons behind their development?

Kamereo's new products are fresh seafood (live shrimp) and ready-to-eat products (pre-washed salads) aimed at busy customers who live alone. The reason for developing these is that restaurants now focus on good-value, low-cost products regardless of brand. Kamereo's angle is that Vietnam has many factories whose production lines aren't optimized, and it puts them to use at 100% capacity.
From outside research, I can see that beyond (1) private label and (2) ready-to-eat, Kamereo also has other categories such as:
(3) Marketplace: third-party manufacturers send goods into Kamereo's warehouse, and Kamereo handles selling, delivery, and payment collection.
(4) Direct imports: Kamereo buys products from overseas suppliers, owns them, and resells them.
