# Kamereo — Business Model & Customer Channels

## Core value proposition
"Restaurants need to order a wide variety of food and supplies every day. We aim to be the one-stop platform that fulfills these needs." Instead of a restaurant juggling dozens of individual wet-market/mom-and-pop suppliers, Kamereo aggregates supply and demand into one ordering platform with reliable, on-time delivery, legal invoicing, and traceability — problems that plague Vietnam's traditionally fragmented, semi-informal food supply chain (no invoices, unstable pricing, late deliveries, no traceability).

Taku Tanaka's framing: **"Price is just one factor of success — never the only one."** Sales reps are told never to lead with a price list; instead, diagnose the customer's actual pain (multi-supplier hassle, no legal invoices, inconsistent quality) and sell the bundle of value, not the SKU price.

## How the supply chain works (vertically integrated, upstream to downstream)
1. **Upstream sourcing** — Kamereo owns/operates vegetable and fruit **collection centers**, working directly with contract farmers (bypasses layers of traditional middlemen). Concrete route: **Đà Lạt → Ho Chi Minh City** on a **"just-in-time" logistics system** specifically to preserve produce quality on the way down from Vietnam's main temperate-vegetable highland region.
2. **Warehousing** — three temperature zones (ambient / chilled / frozen), enabling a broad SKU range including meat and dairy.
3. **Logistics** — own last-mile delivery network; daily refrigerated (cold-chain) transport linking northern and southern Vietnam.
4. **Platform** — web + mobile app for restaurant/buyer ordering; separate supplier-side platform for order management.
5. **Traceability** — every product (fruit, egg tray, etc.) is labeled and traceable back to the farm — a specific differentiator versus wet markets and informal suppliers.

## Customer base (as confirmed by Kamereo's own press materials)
Kamereo describes its customer base as originally restaurant-focused, then expanded to a wider mix:

- **HORECA** (hotels, restaurants, cafés) — the original and core customer base. Includes named accounts like Pizza 4P's, El Gaucho, and luxury hotels. This is the one segment label Kamereo uses itself (its Series B announcement explicitly refers to "the HORECA (hotels, restaurants, and cafes) sector").
- **Supermarkets and convenience stores** — added as urban Vietnam shifts from wet markets to modern retail.
- **Enterprise/institutional accounts** — named example: **Starbucks**, plus factories, schools, and hospitals.
- **Long-tail small businesses** — Taku Tanaka's own framing (Genesia fireside chat) is that reaching billion-dollar scale in Vietnam requires a large sales force penetrating many small accounts individually, since the market lacks Japan/Korea-style national giants (Toyota, Samsung) to anchor a whale-account strategy.

## Newer business lines (2024–2025 Series B expansion)
- **Marketplace model** — Kamereo lets third-party producers/manufacturers store inventory in Kamereo's warehouses while Kamereo handles sales, delivery, and payment collection (an Amazon-FBA-like model for HORECA food supply). First partner: **GYOMU JAPAN / Gyomu Super** (Kobe Bussan Co.) — ~450 products now sellable through Kamereo. This lets small suppliers reach HORECA customers without their own logistics/sales investment, and lets Kamereo monetize its warehouse + last-mile network + traffic without owning the inventory.
- **Private label** — three strategic threads:
  1. Pre-cut vegetables/fruits for supermarkets and convenience stores, riding Vietnam's shift to modern trade and rising demand for food safety/traceability.
  2. Private-labeled consumables manufactured via excess capacity at Vietnamese OEM factories (that normally produce for export) — lower cost, comparable/better quality, strengthens price competitiveness.
  3. **Fresh seafood** — a newer private-label category alongside consumables (per the Vietnam Innovators interview; not yet reflected in written press releases).
- **"Ready-to-eat" line** — pre-washed salads and similar convenience products for busy consumers, extending the private-label push into finished/near-finished goods.
- **Direct imports** — mentioned as a growth area alongside marketplace ops.
- **Geographic expansion** — Hanoi (Dec 2024) complementing HCMC; central Vietnam planned; increasing organic demand from secondary cities.

## Revenue/growth mechanics as described by leadership
- SMBC investor comment: KAMEREO has "created a virtuous growth cycle where acquiring more customers improves procurement conditions, making it easier to attract even more customers" — i.e., demand aggregation → better farmer/supplier pricing → better customer economics → more demand (classic marketplace flywheel).
- KPI philosophy: ultimate KPI is sales, broken into new sales, existing sales, order count, order size, and retention — tracked per rep/team/company and benchmarked against targets, history, and competitors.
