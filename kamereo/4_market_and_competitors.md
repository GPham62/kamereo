# Kamereo — Market & Competitive Landscape

## Market size & structure
- Vietnam's food supply market: **~USD 100 billion**, CAGR **>5%** over the next five years (per Kamereo's own Series B materials).
- Structurally fragmented: in developed markets (Japan, U.S.) large wholesalers and agricultural cooperatives already dominate food distribution; **in Vietnam, no such large players exist** — the market is composed mostly of many individual dealers/traders. This absence of an incumbent is repeatedly cited (by Kamereo and its investors) as the core reason the opportunity is still open.
- No single "clear market leader or winner" as of the Series B round (2025), though Kamereo is described by multiple outlets as **the largest player in Vietnam's B2B food supply e-commerce market.**
- HCMC + Hanoi together account for >50% of Vietnam's GDP — explains why Kamereo's 2-city footprint (until Dec 2024 expansion) still captured outsized market value despite limited geographic coverage.

## Competitors
Vietnam's broader B2B e-commerce / online grocery space includes ~63 startups (Tracxn), but only a handful are funded past seed and directly adjacent to Kamereo's food-focused, HORECA-oriented model:

- **Telio** — Hanoi-based, founded 2018 (same year as Kamereo), B2B grocery platform (spices, instant food, drinks, dry/canned food, dairy, alcohol). Broader FMCG/grocery focus vs. Kamereo's fresh-produce/HORECA specialization. Raised **$64M** across 4 rounds — more total capital than Kamereo, but a different core customer (small retail shops/mom-and-pop stores rather than restaurants).
- **VinShop** — part of the Vingroup ecosystem; also targets SME retail (small shop owners), one of the "first movers" alongside Telio in Vietnam's B2B e-commerce space.
- **ODA** — another funded player in the online grocery / B2B space (limited public detail found).
- Long tail of ~50+ smaller/unfunded B2B e-commerce startups.

**Kamereo's positioning relative to these:** most direct competitors (Telio, VinShop) skew toward general grocery/FMCG distribution to small retail shops. Kamereo's differentiation is its **HORECA-first, vertically integrated fresh-food specialization** — owning upstream collection centers and cold-chain logistics, rather than just aggregating dry-goods distribution. This is a narrower but deeper niche (perishables, food-safety, traceability) that is harder to replicate than dry-goods resale.

## Notable customers (signals of market position)
- **Starbucks** (Vietnam) — global-brand enterprise account.
- **El Gaucho**, **Pizza 4P's** — premium/well-known F&B chains.
- Luxury hotels.
- Supermarkets, convenience stores, factories, schools, hospitals — i.e., Kamereo has expanded well beyond its original single-restaurant wedge.
- First marketplace partner: **GYOMU JAPAN / Gyomu Super** (Kobe Bussan Co.) — ~450 products.

## Recognition
- **Forbes Asia "100 to Watch" (2025)**.
- Backed by a mix of Southeast-Asia-focused VC (Genesia Ventures, Quest Ventures, CPF Group/C.P. Vietnam) and, in the Series B, major Japanese corporates/financial institutions (Sumitomo Corporation, SMBC Venture Capital, Mitsubishi UFJ Capital, Inspire Co., REAZON HOLDINGS) — signals both financial and potential strategic/retail-partnership backing (e.g., Sumitomo's Fujimart retail business in Hanoi named explicitly as a future collaboration channel).
